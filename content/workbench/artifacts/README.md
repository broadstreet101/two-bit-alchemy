# Artifact Draft Workflow

Artifact drafts begin here before they are ready for Cabinet entries, exhibits, Field Notes, Workshop Journal entries, or publication.

This directory is part of the Workbench described in `content/workbench/README.md`. Nothing here is considered published.

## Purpose

An artifact draft captures what is known, what is remembered, what is uncertain, and what still needs review.

The goal is to preserve raw material without forcing it into polished prose too early.

## Rules

- Capture first.
- Organize lightly.
- Preserve uncertainty.
- Do not invent missing facts.
- Do not replace the author's voice.
- Keep technical implementation separate from editorial development.
- Treat every artifact draft as unpublished until it graduates through review.

## Required Sections

Each artifact draft should contain:

- Artifact Name
- Current Status
- Physical Description
- Provenance (how it came into my possession)
- Story
- Why I Kept It
- Why It Matters
- Related People
- Related Places
- Related Exhibits
- Related Field Notes
- Related Workshop Journal entries
- Historical Context
- Publication Notes
- Open Questions
- Media Needed
- Source Material
- Editorial Notes

## Graduation Criteria

An artifact draft may move forward when:

- Known facts are separated from open questions.
- Personal story material has been reviewed by Dada.
- Images, captions, permissions, and credits are identified where needed.
- The artifact can answer the relevant Exhibit Standard questions.
- The next destination is clear: Cabinet, Exhibit, Field Note, Workshop Journal entry, or continued Workbench review.
